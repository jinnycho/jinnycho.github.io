🏖 Summer vacation in Scandinavian countries
Aug 10, 2019
5 minute read

This summer, I spent a week in Copenhagen, Denmark and 4 cities in west Norway. I’d like to share some thoughts & photos.

🇩🇰 Denmark

Copenhagen

It was interesting to see so many bicycles all over the city. It makes sense because there’s practically no hill or mountain in Copenhagen. But they also have very organized road systems to encourage people to use them, which is great. The city itself was very clean as well with no rats running in the metro or dead cockroaches on the streets. Another thing that caught my eyes was there’s no insect screen behind the windows.

☝️ It is a Copenhagen public library. I really loved how clean and organized the public library was. If I really live in Copenhagen, I would definitely go there everyday to work or read books. The chairs were also very comfy and the comics were kind of dark considering they were in the children’s section.

☝️ Lego is one of the most famous toys in the world produced from Denmark.

☝️ This is the Frederiksborg Castle. It was my first time actually seeing a real castle (I haven’t been to Europe before) and this castle was beautiful with all the engravings, furniture and marbles. However, I thought the gardens are too artificial and egocentric. For example, the shapes of the trees are rectangles or triangles. All the flowers or trees are strictly tied in chains to create those shapes. I could actually enjoy the park when I looked at it from the castle instead of being inside there. I think I prefer the natural beauty of the garden like I usually see in Korean palaces or houses. (example below: Changdeokgung secret palace). But I liked that dogs are allowed in part of the Frederiksborg castle 🐕.

☝️ This is a very well-known Nyhavn waterfront.

Also visited Illums Bolighus. I spent hours from there. I am a big admirer of good interior design and furniture. They had some of the finest lightings, furniture and homeware. It was very inspiring to see how Danish people decorate their places.

It was nice to see young people of my age (or younger) working in the markets. In Korea, people working in the markets are usually pretty old and rough . But here, these people were very polished, young and looked happy. I believe it could be related to free & well-established education systems here. But I was also impressed that there seems no big discrimination based on occupations. (I should admit that inequality & discrimination still do exist in Korea especially by their income & arbitrary social status people set. eg. The less manual work, the better 🤷‍♂️)

🇳🇴 Norway

Flåm

Flåm was one of the most beautiful places in Norway, where I can tour Sognefjords. We rode a ferry and I liked that it was an electric ferry which had makes less polution on nature.

☝️ The weather was perfect and it was stunning. I took this photo because it looked like a human lying on the ground. It was quite windy and a little cold but I was outside almost the whole 2 hours because I couldn’t stop watching these magnificent views. I thought I could even write a novel inspired on this view. It also made me feel humble and relaxed.

Another thing that came into my mind was this word “愚公移山”. It means a fool that moves the mountain. I sometimes get very depressed and feel like I’m useless. But if I just keep concentrating and accomplish small thing at a time without giving up, I might be able to create something quite beautiful as this. This fjord wouldn’t be created in a day. Everything I dream takes time.

Voss

This was a city on the way to Bergen and it was like a sudden gift. We found very new and sturdy cable cars that go to the top of the mountains. On this peak, there was a good restaurant and we can see the whole view of the city.

Stavanger

I heard Stavanger is one of the developed cities in Norway and it is also known for Preikestolen, the rock where people can see the beautiful fjords. Unfortunately, the weather was awful and we were completely wet and pretty much couldnt’t see anything from this rock. However, I still appreciate hiking there. The past few days had been wonderful so I felt like the mountain is telling me: “The life is not always sunny!”

This hotel was very nice. It had stoves, w/d and pretty much everything you need. It was like a real house with beautiful scandinavian styles. I really recommend.

A cool statue of a man with braided beard and a beer. 😆

Conclusion

As someone who visited Europe for the first time, I think it was a great decision to go to Denmark and Norway. It was not only cool (both in terms of temperature and city) and beautiful, the people were friendly and food was great. Especially in summer, I heard the other cities are very crowded and it would’ve been very depressing if I lost my wallet or camera or got lost in the city where there are unhappy people. This trip also helped me reflect my life and was very inspiring.
